### Psychological/Structural Explanation

**Cited Research**:
- **Frustration-induced purchases**: Candy Crush's business model relies on "pinch levels" designed to induce frustration, triggering $0.99 extra moves purchases ([Naavik: Candy Crushing It](https://naavik.co/deep-dives/candy-crush-10-year-reign/)).
- **FOMO/Time pressure**: Wordscapes' 13-week tournament seasons create scarcity through time pressure, shown by sawtooth revenue graphs peaking during events ([AppLovin](https://www.applovin.com/blog/how-wordscapes-improved-ltv-hybrid-monetization-trends/)).
- **Sunk cost fallacy**: Players near level completion (1 move away) feel loss aversion, triggering $0.99 purchase to avoid "wasted" effort.
- **Social proof/competition**: Leaderboards drive competitive spending; players spend to maintain rank during final tournament hours.

### What Backfires

1. **Pay-to-win in competitive modes**: 41% of high-spending players report they will reduce spending due to P2W frustration; competitive players resent buying victories over skill.
2. **Too many hard levels in sequence**: Candy Crush balances easy:hard ratios carefully; too many consecutive hard levels = rage quit.
3. **Deceptive pricing**: 45% of puzzle gamers report post-purchase regret due to "limited utility or impact in the game".
4. **Ads in FTUE**: Serving ads during onboarding increases churn; best practice is no ads in first 30 minutes.
5. **Penalty box over-monetization**: WordRun's 15-960 minute penalty tiers risk churn if players feel punished unfairly; must offer non-monetary escape (watch 3 rewarded videos to reduce wait time).

### Engagement Blueprint by Genre

#### Word Puzzle Games (Wordscapes, Word Cookies)

**Long Session Drivers** (20 min avg):
1. **Flow loops**: Gradual difficulty curve; players solve 5-10 levels in flow state before hitting hard level.
2. **Chaining levels**: Auto-start next level after completion (reduce friction).
3. **Sticker collection**: Wordscapes awards stickers every 5 levels; collect 3 to customize leaderboard avatar.
4. **Tournament sessions**: 13-week seasons drive extended play during competitive windows.

**Return Drivers**:
1. **Daily quests**: Wordscapes daily puzzles + streak bonuses.
2. **Tournament leaderboard resets**: Weekly/monthly resets create fresh competitive cycles.
3. **Limited-time events**: Seasonal events (Halloween, Thanksgiving) drive 25% revenue spikes.
4. **Lives regeneration**: 5 lives, 1 refill per 30 min = return in 2.5 hours.

**D1/D7/D30 Correlation**:
- Wordscapes achieves D1 31-33% via strong FTUE (no ads in first 30 min, freebies for tutorial completion).
- Leaderboard integration lifts D30 by 26%.

#### Match-3 Puzzle Games (Candy Crush, Royal Match)

**Long Session Drivers** (28.97 min avg):
1. **Infinite retry**: Lives system allows 5 attempts before wait time; players chain attempts until lives depleted.
2. **Pinch-easy-easy-pinch rhythm**: Candy Crush uses difficulty waves; 1 hard level, 3 easy, 1 hard.
3. **Meta progression**: Homescapes/Gardenscapes add home decoration loop; players solve puzzles to earn stars for story progression.
4. **Social pressure**: Guild events require daily contributions; fear of letting team down extends sessions.

**Return Drivers**:
1. **Lives regeneration**: Return every 2.5 hours for full lives refill.
2. **Daily spin/rewards**: Royal Match daily reward wheel.
3. **Event calendar**: Candy Crush runs overlapping events (treasure hunt, team challenges, seasonal themes); average 2-3 concurrent events.
4. **Leaderboard resets**: Weekly leaderboards for guild ranking.

**D1/D7/D30 Correlation**:
- Match-3 achieves 24-25% D1, 6.6% D30 (highest in puzzle category).
- Guild mechanics add 26% retention lift at D30.

#### Hybrid-Casual Puzzle Games (Block Blast, Hexa Sort)

**Long Session Drivers** (13 min avg):
1. **Infinite loop**: No lives system; players can play indefinitely (ad-gated).
2. **Quick rounds**: 30-60 second puzzles; players complete 10-20 in a session.
3. **Streaks**: Combo multipliers for consecutive clears; players chase high scores.

**Return Drivers**:

## Limits of Evidence & Research Gaps

### Acknowledged Data Gaps

1. **Word-Chain Subcategory Sizing**: No granular market size data found specifically for "typing-based word-chain" games. Puzzle genre is well-documented ($12.2bn), but word-chain niche within word puzzles is underreported. **Inference**: Small but growing niche based on presence of apps like Word Chain Puzzle, Words Chain Reaction.

2. **Typing Input Retention Impact**: No studies found quantifying retention impact of typo forgiveness in mobile typing games. **Inference based on UX research**: Mobile typing friction reduces engagement 15-20%; typo forgiveness likely mitigates 50-70% of this friction (10-15% net retention lift estimate).

3. **Penalty Box Monetization**: No benchmarks found for "pay to skip wait time" in puzzle games. Candy Crush lives system is closest analog, but penalty box (15-960 min) is more punitive. **High risk of churn**; recommend A/B test 50% of users with penalty box, 50% without to measure impact.

4. **Regional Word Game Performance**: Data heavily skewed toward US (78% of word game revenue). Asian market performance unknown due to language barriers in word puzzles. WordRun may face geographic ceiling unless localized to non-English markets.

5. **Competitive Word-Chain Market Size**: No revenue data found for existing word-chain games (Word Chain Puzzle, Words Chain Reaction, Connect Word). **Inference**: Niche is fragmented with no clear leader, suggesting blue ocean opportunity OR low market demand (unclear which).

### Conflicting Sources

- **Puzzle game CPI**: Mapendo reports iOS $3, Android $2; Business of Apps reports iOS $4.50, Android $1.10. **Resolution**: Use $3-4.50 iOS, $1.10-2 Android range; test actual CPI in soft launch.
- **Mobile game IAP revenue 2024**: Sensor Tower reports $81bn (one source) and $82bn (another source). **Resolution**: Likely due to different report timing or methodology; use $80.9bn as consensus.

### Forecasts vs Actuals

- **2024 actuals** are well-documented (Sensor Tower, AppMagic, GameAnalytics).
- **2025-2026 forecasts** are moderate confidence; based on 4% CAGR from 2024 trends.
- **2027-2030 forecasts** are low confidence; extrapolated from industry sentiment, not hard data.
